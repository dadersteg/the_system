# SAPGS \- Structured Approach to Personal Goal Setting

## Objective:

To guide users in systematically setting and achieving personal goals, ensuring clarity, balance, and adaptability.

## Level 1 Goals (Primary Goals):

### Clarity and Realism:

* Goals must be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).  
* Regularly review and update goals to ensure they remain relevant and achievable.

### Balance:

* Incorporate goals from various life aspects, including career, personal growth, and health.  
* Regularly assess and adjust focus to maintain a holistic approach.

## Level 2 Sub-goals (Intermediate Objectives):

### Alignment:

* Each sub-goal must directly contribute to the primary goals.  
* Continuously ensure alignment with primary goals, adjusting as necessary.

### Flexibility:

* Be prepared to modify sub-goals in response to life changes while focusing on primary goals.  
* Regularly evaluate and adjust sub-goals to reflect current circumstances and priorities.

## Level 3 Methods (Actionable Steps):

### Feasibility and Sustainability:

* Choose practical, sustainable, and easily integrated methods into daily routines.  
* Regularly reassess methods to ensure they remain effective and manageable.

### Tracking and Adaptation:

* Implement a system for tracking progress towards goals.  
* Use tracking insights to adapt methods, ensuring they are the most effective for the individual’s context.

## Level 4 Habits 

### Building Effective Habits:

* Emphasise incremental improvements for massive gains. Small, consistent actions compound over time, leading to significant outcomes.  
* Implement the four laws of behaviour change to form new habits: Make it obvious, make it attractive, make it easy, and make it satisfying. This framework helps create an environment where good habits are automatic and bad habits are difficult to continue.  
* Practice habit stacking by linking new habits to established routines. This method simplifies the incorporation of new behaviours by piggybacking on existing habits.  
* Focus on the process rather than the outcome. Consistent daily improvements are more beneficial for long-term success than sporadic efforts.  
* Establish accountability mechanisms, such as habit tracking or partnerships, to enhance commitment and visibility of progress.

### Adapting and Growing with Habits:

* Conduct periodic reviews of your habits to ensure they align with your goals and adapt to changing circumstances. This flexibility allows you to phase out habits that no longer serve you and introduce new, more relevant behaviours.  
* Align habits with your identity rather than just goal achievement. Focusing on becoming the type of person who achieves these goals naturally leads to more sustainable habit formation and goal attainment.

## Usage Instructions for an LLM:

* When assessing a user's approach to habit formation, guide them through the four laws of behaviour change for creating compelling, lasting habits.  
* Provide specific strategies for habit stacking and emphasise the importance of focusing on small, daily actions that lead to significant long-term results.  
* Encourage users to align their habits with their identity, helping them see the intrinsic value in their adopting behaviours.  
* Advise on the importance of regular habit review and adjustment to align with the user’s evolving circumstances, goals, and identity.
